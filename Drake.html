Early Life

When Tim Drake was very young, he went with his parents to watch the Flying Graysons perform. It was the same performance where Dick Grayson's parents were killed and he was adopted by Bruce Wayne. By the age of 9 Tim learned that the secret identity of Robin was Dick Grayson, after seeing Robin perform a quadruple somersault that he recognized from Dick’s performance as a Flying Grayson, and also made the connection of Bruce being Batman. This inspired Tim to work on his own acrobatics and detctive skills, though he didn’t have any intention at the time to work with Batman. Tim would later notice when Dick Grayson became Nightwing and Jason Todd took over as the new Robin. After Jason died at the hands of the Joker, Tim noticed that Batman was becoming more angry and aggresive, and came to the conclusion that he needed a Robin to help him from becoming to enveloped in crime fighting. Tim went to Dick Grayson to convince him to become Robin again, but Dick had put that part of his life behind him, so Tim went to Wayne Manor with Dick and basically told Bruce that he was going to be the new Robin. After some convincing, Bruce eventually agreed, and Tim became the third official Robin.

Life as Robin

While serving the same overall purpose as both Dick and Jason had, Tim proved to be an incredible detective while working with Batman, almost equal and with the potential to be better than Batman himself. Tim was the first Robin to seek out Batman, instead of being adopted, and had less issues with Batman as they worked together, though Batman himself most likley also improved after the previous two Robins.

Red Robin

When Batman died (he gets better, its comics) Dick Grayson took over the role of Batman, and didn’t want Tim to work under him, beliving that they were equals, and instead wanted to train Damian Wayne, Bruce’s biological son, as Robin. Tim took the name “Red Robin” (no relation to the restaurant) and donning a new costume, operated as his own hero, while also searching for Bruce, who he belived to still be alive. He eventually found him, but even though Bruce became Batman once again, Tim remained Red Robin, and Bruce continued to train Damian.
